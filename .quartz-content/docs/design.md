Shomosite borrows the design discipline of [Gwern](https://gwern.net): serious typography, minimal friction, dense but readable navigation, and a refusal to bury writing beneath decorative interface noise. The point is not homage. The point is to keep the reading lane clear and make the knowledge graph feel native to the page rather than bolted on afterward.

The public site is also designed to remain honest about absence. If a published note refers to a private note such as [[shomopedia/private-life]] or an unpublished draft such as [[fiction/long-project]], the public rendering should preserve the sentence while refusing to pretend that the destination is public.
