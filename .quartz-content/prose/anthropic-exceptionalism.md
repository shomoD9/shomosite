Human beings are interesting not because we are the strongest animals, but because we can create explanatory knowledge that no genome directly encoded for us. That is the discontinuity I mean by anthropic exceptionalism. It is not mysticism. It is the simple observation that criticism, conjecture, and cultural accumulation let us reach beyond our starting conditions.

The practical consequence is that AI should not be discussed merely as a labor substitute. It should be discussed as a new participant in the ecology through which explanatory knowledge is made, challenged, and reorganized.
