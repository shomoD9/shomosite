---
title: Working together
eyebrow: Notes on collaboration
summary: Sometimes the public work turns into private work. When it does, I prefer a short, well-scoped piece of work over something open-ended.
ctaLabel: Write to me
ctaSubject: Working together
supportNote: Send a short note with context, what feels unclear, and why it matters now.
process:
  - A short note or introduction starts the conversation.
  - If there is a fit, we define the question, scope, and timeline directly.
  - The work happens in a focused window and is handed off cleanly.
  - A short follow-up window covers loose ends.
fit:
  - You have a concrete question, project, or bottleneck.
  - You want clarity and execution more than a long process.
  - You are comfortable with a scoped piece of work.
notFit:
  - Ongoing management or content at volume.
  - Open-ended hourly advisory without a defined question.
  - Large-organization process with too many moving approvals.
---
Most of what matters on this site is public. Sometimes that public work leads to a private conversation. When it does, I prefer to keep the arrangement simple: define the question well, do the work carefully, and leave behind something useful.

That keeps the relationship honest. The work should solve a real problem or clarify a real decision. It should not be designed to create permanent dependency.
