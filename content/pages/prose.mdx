Prose is where the practice becomes explicit. It is where an argument, sensibility, or claim gets tested in language until it can carry real weight.

Sometimes that means essays and public writing. Sometimes it means a voice document, a homepage, or a piece of narrative framing that lets a person or body of work sound like itself again.

The standard here is not warmth, volume, or polish for its own sake. It is precision. The language should clarify what is being said, what matters in it, and why it deserves attention.
