Product is where the practice gets tested against use. The question is not only whether something can be built, but what claim it is making, for whom, and why it should exist in the first place.

The public side of that work appears here as product writing, conceptual framing, and small builds. The private side, when it exists, is usually about clarifying scope, positioning, or the shape of a decision before waste compounds.

What matters most is removing avoidable ambiguity. A product should know what it is, what it changes, and why its form makes sense. Everything else gets easier once that is true.
